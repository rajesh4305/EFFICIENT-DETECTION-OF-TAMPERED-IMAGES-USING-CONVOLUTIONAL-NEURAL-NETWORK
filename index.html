About Our Technology

Our image forgery detection system uses Convolutional Neural Networks (CNNs) to identify manipulated images with 99.85% validation accuracy and 93.33% overall classification accuracy.

The system can detect various types of image tampering including:

  • Copy-move forgery
  • Image splicing
  • Retouching
  • Resampling
  • Morphing

Using advanced preprocessing techniques like Error Level Analysis (ELA), image denoising, and Gaussian noise injection, our model outperforms traditional methods and even RNN-based approaches.

Research & Methodology

Our Approach

1

Data Collection

Using the CASIA 2.0 Image Tampering Detection Dataset with 7,491 authentic and tampered images.

2

Preprocessing

Applying Error Level Analysis (ELA), image resizing, denoising, and Gaussian noise injection.

3

CNN Architecture

Implementing a specialized CNN with convolutional layers, pooling, and dropout for robust feature extraction.

4

Training & Evaluation

Training on augmented data and evaluating using precision, recall, accuracy, and F1-score metrics.
